# Game 3 Incentives

Ready for more? Let's go through this group by group.

## Group A 🐊

Mexico has clinched 1st place in Group A, and a R32 match in CDMX, and has nothing at stake on Match Day 3. Along with Canada and the U.S., they've demonstrated the power of home-field advantage in the World Cup. They have a very Anglo-centric path to the quarterfinals with Scotland and England as their most likely opponents in the R32 and R16, respectively. Those two games will be played in Mexico, though, which is a tough assignment for any opponent as Mexico has one of the larger home-field advantages in the world.

South Korea, with 3 points, is essentially guaranteed to advance with a win or a draw. Basically, 4 points in the group stage will be enough to get you in, even if it requires the 3rd-place backdoor route. What about 3 points? We currently project that most teams with 3 points will get in, but some won't. For ranking the 3rd-place teams[6], the first tiebreaker is goal differential (GD), and South Korea is currently at 0, which means they'd have a negative GD if they lose to South Africa. That could get dicey. They were a bit unlucky in the match against Mexico, but they ought to beat South Africa, though.

Czechia, with 1 point, would almost certainly advance with a win, but that's a tough ask against Mexico at Estadio Azteca. Were they to draw instead, there's an outside chance that a team with 2 points could advance, but it would require a lot of things to break perfectly. It helps that Czechia's GD is only -1. While Czechia are significant underdogs, the good news is that their most likely R32 opponent, conditional on advancing, is relatively beatable (Egypt).

South Africa faces essentially the same situation, although with a draw being even less likely to advance them because of their -2 GD. It's basically win-or-bust against South Korea.

## Group B 🐝

Canada and Switzerland, each with 4 points, will play one another and are in a weird zone where they've practically clinched with probability >=99.9%, but not technically so. Both teams would be absolutely guaranteed to advance on a draw, which, in theory, should lead to more conservative play.[7] There's also first place in the group at stake; because Canada has the superior GD, they'd take the 1-slot in the event of a draw. The most likely R32 opponent for the 1st-place finisher is Algeria or Iran, and for the 2nd-place finisher is South Korea.

Bosnia-Herzegovina and Qatar, each with 1 point, are essentially playing an elimination match. It's very likely that the winner will advance, and it's extremely unlikely that a draw would be enough, especially with both teams having poor GDs. They really have an incentive to go for it. The winner will probably face the United States in the R32.

## Group C 🦀

Brazil, with 4 points, is on the long list of teams that have practically but not technically clinched advancement. Even a win against Scotland wouldn't officially guarantee that they win the group, however.

That's because Morocco, also with 4 points including a draw against Brazil, could lap them on goal differential by running up the score against Haiti. The most likely R32-R16 path for the 1st-place team in Group C is Japan-Norway and for the 2nd-place team is Netherlands-Switzerland. It's not clear which route is better, so maybe this isn't one of the higher-stakes matches.

Scotland, with 3 points, can still technically win the group, but the far more likely outcome considering their opponent (Brazil) is that they finish in 3rd. Their goal is basically to avoid a multi-goal loss, which could make the tiebreakers hairy.

Haiti has been eliminated.

## Group D 🐬

The United States has advanced and won the group, having been very good in the process. The match against Turkey is irrelevant other than for vibes/momentum-building. If you're considering betting on the match, note that the U.S. may sit players with yellow cards to avoid a potential second yellow and suspension in the R32. The U.S. is in a pretty favorable part of the bracket, with Bosnia-Herzegovina and Egypt as the most likely R32 and R16 opponents. However, we'd then potentially draw Spain in the quarterfinals in what would probably be the most important match in American soccer history.

Australia and Paraguay, each with 3 points, will play one another in a match with slightly weird incentives. Australia is a heavy favorite to advance no matter what, but Paraguay, with a poor GD because of its blowout loss to the U.S., really wants that 4th point. The game could be drawish, especially with neither team having a chance to win the group.

Turkey has been eliminated, and there's no presidential pardon coming even if they're going by their alias Turkiye.

## Group E 🦅

Germany has advanced and clinched the group. They're slotted to play a 3rd-place team in the R32, which, more likely than not, will be the 3rd-place finisher in Group D, so Paraguay or Australia. However, the Group E and Group I winners could face one another in the R16, meaning Germany will likely run into France then.

Cote d'Ivoire is in a strong position with 3 points and a final match against a weaker side in Curacao. A win would guarantee 2nd place in the group; they can't bypass Germany having lost head-to-head. But no route here is particularly easy: as a 2nd-place finisher, their most likely R32 opponent would be PELE favorite Norway, while qualifying as a 3rd-place team could give them Mexico at Estadio Azteca.

Ecuador, which tends to be a favorite of PELE and other statistical models, has been a disappointment so far and probably deserves better than their 1 point. They're pretty much in a win-or-go-home situation against Germany.

Curacao, likewise, is in a win-or-bust position. Their GD is terrible because of their 7-1 loss to Germany. But a win would give them 4 points, and that would probably be enough regardless of GD.

## Group F 🦊

The Netherlands will play Tunisia in a match that Nate will be attending in Kansas City. With 4 points, the Netherlands has effectively clinched advancement, but there is something at stake for winning the group, as the most likely R32-R16 path for the Group F winner is Morocco-Switzerland versus the more difficult Brazil-Norway for the 2nd-place Group F team.

Japan is in a parallel position. Both the Netherlands and Japan have 4 points, and they drew their head-to-head match. Both teams also have a +4 GD. The next tiebreaker is goals scored, where the Netherlands is one goal ahead of Japan. Should it come down to it, Japan has a real incentive to continue pressing the accelerator against Sweden.

Sweden themselves are also in a good position to advance, though, after a pair of rather weird results, beating Tunisia 5-1 but losing to the Netherlands by the exact same scoreline. A win against Japan would advance them; a draw almost certainly would, too; and even a loss might be good enough, since their tiebreakers are relatively favorable due to their high number of goals scored.

Tunisia has been eliminated, however.

## Group G 🦒

This group is a mess relative to pre-tournament expectations. But Egypt, like other 4-point teams, is essentially guaranteed to advance. They'd finish first in the group with a win against Iran, or probably a draw, although Belgium could lap them with a lopsided scoreline against New Zealand.

Belgium, with just 1 goal scored so far, indeed an own-goal by Egypt, has a lot to prove. But their advancement scenarios are simple. A win will almost certainly be good enough. A draw probably works too, putting them on 3 points, all on draws, with a 0 GD.[8] A loss to New Zealand probably won't work, though, and would prompt some deeper questions about the team. On the bright side, Belgium could still claim the group with a win. How much does that matter? The 1st-place team will probably play USA in the R16, while the 2nd-place finisher is on Argentina's path. So yes, first place in Group G matters, unless you're really a believer in the USMNT.

Iran is in a similar position to Belgium, though with an incrementally better tiebreaker scenario because they have one more goal scored than the Red Devils. They also have a 15 percent chance of winning the group, which could potentially set up an Iran-vs.-USA clash in the R16.

Finally, New Zealand, with 1 point, is basically another win-or-go-home team. On paper, the assignment against Belgium is difficult, but we'll see if Belgium continues to underachieve.

## Group H 🦔

It's very important to finish first in this group because the 2nd-place finisher will draw Argentina in the R32, whereas the 1st-place team's most likely R32 opponent is Austria. Spain currently tops the group and would clinch 1st with a win against Uruguay. A draw would very probably be enough too, given their superior GD vs. Cape Verde.[9]

Uruguay is arguably in the highest-leverage spot in the tournament. There's only a 15 percent chance of a win against Spain, according to our model, even though it tends to like the smaller South American countries relative to the consensus. Even a win wouldn't clinch first place, though, because Cape Verde could leapfrog them with a superior GD against Saudi Arabia. What about a draw against Spain? That would put Uruguay on 3 points, all from draws, which would probably be good enough for the tiebreakers given that their matches have been relatively high-scoring so far. A 1-goal loss against Spain would even leave them with an outside chance. Here's where it gets weird, though: Uruguay would rather finish 3rd in the group than 2nd, because the 2nd-place finisher's R32 opponent would be Argentina.

Underdogs Cape Verde are actually in decent shape to advance too, with a 71 percent chance. A win against Saudi Arabia would clinch advancement, and a draw would likely be good enough as well. In fact, our simulations have Cape Verde finishing 2nd in the group a majority of the time. Unfortunately, that means Spain is their most likely R32 opponent.

Saudi Arabia, with 1 point, is another win-or-bust team. Even if a 2-point team sneaks in, should they draw with Cape Verde, it almost certainly won't be Saudi Arabia given their inferior GD.

## Group I 🦎

France and Norway have both clinched advancement with 6 points each, but the top spot in Group I is up for grabs. You could argue about how meaningful it is, though. The 1st-place team in Group I probably gets Sweden-Germany in the R32-R16, whereas the 2nd-place path probably runs through Cote d'Ivoire-Brazil. The math on who wins the group is simple, though. It will be France with a win or draw; the late goal that Norway conceded to Senegal gives the GD tiebreaker to France. Norway will finish first with a win, however.

Senegal had the misfortune of being placed in what is probably the toughest group, but the good news is that they've yet to play the one weak spot, Iraq. Even with 0 points so far, they're 94 percent to advance conditional on beating Iraq. Because this would necessarily require the 3rd-place path, France and Norway will finish 1st and 2nd in some order, goal differential is extremely important, so expect Senegal to pile on the goals if they can. However, their most likely R32 opponent would be England.

Iraq, meanwhile, is in the category that our model describes as "needs miracle". I don't mind how they've played, but the country has never earned a point in a World Cup match. Should they beat Senegal, only a 9 percent chance, they'll still face the problem that their GD is an extremely poor -6, so they'd be one of the 3-point teams that could lose the game of musical chairs.

## Group J 🐆

Argentina has clinched the group and advancement after 5 Lionel Messi goals. They don't have anything to play for, but they will be scoreboard-watching. That's because the Group J winner has one of the most difficult R32 assignments, facing the 2nd-place team in Group H. That figured to be Uruguay or possibly Spain, but for now, the most likely #2 from Group H is Cape Verde.

Austria and Algeria will play one another, meanwhile, each sitting on 3 points. They have an extremely strong incentive to play for a draw in Kansas City because 4 points will very probably be enough to advance, whereas 3 points might not be, especially for Algeria with its -2 GD, and neither team can win the group in any event.

Jordan didn't play poorly, but has been eliminated.

## Group K 🐨

This one is relatively simple. Colombia tops Group K now and has clinched advancement with 6 points, but they haven't won the group yet. They'll take the #1 slot with a win or draw against Portugal, and the 1-seed in Group K has a considerably more favorable path, most likely Ghana-Canada in the R32-R16 rather than Croatia-Spain.

Portugal, however, has a lot to prove even after a 5-0 win against Uzbekistan on Tuesday. They'll claim the group with a win against Colombia but not otherwise. With 4 points, they're almost certain to advance, but winning the group is still important. Although Portugal could technically finish in 3rd place instead, that's not a favorable route either, with the probable R32-R16 matchups being England-Mexico.

DR Congo has just 1 point, but they have an opportunity to add more because their MD3 opponent is Uzbekistan. Their GD is only -1, so like other teams, they should advance if they win the match and finish with 4 points.

Uzbekistan, meanwhile, isn't technically eliminated despite having 0 points and an awful GD, but they need a lot of help. A win against DR Congo would clinch 3rd place in Group K, but they'll probably need a multiple-goal win to avoid losing out on tiebreakers.

## Group L 🦁

England, after a draw against a Ghana team that was very much playing for the tie, is essentially guaranteed advancement. But winning the group matters: the most likely R32 opponent for the Group L winner is DR Congo, while the 2nd-place team will probably play Portugal and then Spain in the R16. England is heavily favored against Panama and has an incentive to pile on the score. They have a +2 GD versus Ghana's +1 and are also ahead on goals scored, so if both teams win, England would probably finish 1st in the group. What about with a draw? Actually, that's not great for them. If England draw and the Ghana-Croatia match is also a draw, they'll win the group. But if England draw and there's a winner in Ghana-Croatia, the victor will overtake them.

Ghana is one of the more overrated teams in the tournament according to PELE, and I'm not sure their performance has really overcome the model's doubts. They were probably inferior to Panama and won on a late goal, and were certainly the worse side against England and recognized as much. However, with 4 points, they're almost certainly in the knockout round. They might as well go for it against Croatia, since, as we've covered, the 2nd position in Group L has a highly unfavorable knockout-stage draw, although advancing from 3rd from the group would be comparatively better, most likely Colombia-Canada in R32-R16.

Croatia, which reached the final in 2018 and the semifinal in 2022, has been one of those teams where you could easily take a glass-half-full or half-empty view based on their 3 points so far. They'll advance with a win against Ghana and almost certainly also advance with a draw, and a loss might be good enough also given that their tiebreakers are relatively favorable. But Croatia will win the group if they beat Ghana and England draws or loses, and there's a big reward for finishing 1st rather than 2nd in Group L.

Panama has been eliminated, but will be looking for its first-ever World Cup point against England.

## Notes

[1] Although it does so imperfectly. Because 3rd-place qualifiers are selected from among all groups, teams in the latter groups will have a better grasp on their incentives.

[2] In the group stage, a win is worth 3 points and a draw is worth 1.

[3] It requires having lost out against other prospective 3rd-place teams in the group based on head-to-head results.

[4] Conditional odds also reflect the informational value of each match. Other things being equal, a team winning will tend to increase its rating in the model.

[5] Although it's technically, probabilistically possible for the same opponent to be the most likely in multiple rounds, I've instructed the model not to select an opponent again once it's already been chosen.

[6] Head-to-head results are now FIFA's first tiebreaker, but they don't apply to inter-group rankings.

[7] Indeed, our model adjusts its expected scoreline in cases where both teams would be guaranteed to advance with draw, or both teams are win-or-bust, although in practice, the mechanics of the 32-knockout round make these scenarios less likely.

[8] There are two ways to finish the group stage with 3 points: either 3 draws, or 1 win and 2 losses. The 3-draw path necessarily implies a GD of 0. Most teams with 1 win and 2 losses will have a negative GD, however.

[9] If Spain draws and Cape Verde wins, both teams would finish with 5 points; since Cape Verde improbably tied Spain, 1st place would come down to GD.
